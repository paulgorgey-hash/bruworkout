1
Barbell Hip Thrust
King of glute mass — non-negotiable
5 sets 6–8 reps
▾
🍑 Glute Focus: The hip thrust is THE #1 exercise for glute mass because it puts the glute maximus under maximum tension at full hip extension — the exact range where glutes produce the most force. Every rep at the top = maximum growth signal.
✅ Key Coaching Cues
Bar sits on hip crease, padded. Both feet flat, shoulder-width, toes slightly out.
Upper back (NOT neck/shoulder blades) rests on bench edge. Keep chin tucked toward chest.
Drive THROUGH YOUR HEELS. Feel the heel pressure throughout the whole movement.
At the top: body is a straight line from knees to shoulders. Squeeze glutes maximally for 1–2 seconds.
Posterior pelvic tilt at the top — tuck your tailbone under, don't hyperextend your lower back.
Lower controlled in 2–3 seconds. Do NOT let hips drop fast.
❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid
Pushing through toes — shifts load to quads, kills glute activation.
Hyperextending lower back at top — you want glute squeeze, not lumbar arch.
Rushing the concentric — control equals tension equals growth.
Knees caving in — keep them in line with toes, push them out slightly.

Romanian Deadlift (RDL)
Hamstring-glute lengthening — slow eccentric
4 sets 8–10 reps
▾
🍑 Glute Focus: The RDL creates a long-length glute stimulus — it stretches the glute maximus AND hamstrings under load. Research shows stretched-position training creates more muscle damage and hypertrophy signal. The 3-second eccentric multiplies this effect dramatically.
✅ Key Coaching Cues
Start standing, bar or dumbbells at hips. Soft bend in knees — maintain this throughout.
HINGE at the hips, not the waist. Send your hips BACK, not your torso down.
Lower for 3 full seconds. Feel the deep stretch in your hamstrings/glutes at the bottom.
Go to just below knee level (or until you feel a strong stretch) — don't round your back.
Drive hips FORWARD to return. Squeeze glutes at the top — not just standing up straight.
Keep bar close to your legs the entire movement. Drag it up your shins mentally.
❌ Common Mistakes
Squatting the weight — this is a HINGE, not a squat. Knees should barely move.
Rounding the lower back — arch maintained throughout. Spine is neutral.
Going too fast — the 3-second descent is the most important part for glute growth.

1
Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch
Priority #1 — unlocks glute activation
3 rounds60 sec each
▾
🍑 Why this is #1: Reciprocal inhibition — when your hip flexor is shortened and tight (from sitting), your nervous system automatically TURNS DOWN the glute on the opposite side. This is why many people squat and feel it only in their quads. Stretch the hip flexor = unlock the glute. Do this EVERY DAY.
✅ How to Stretch
Kneel on one knee (right knee down, left foot forward). Place a folded mat under the knee for comfort.
Shift your hips FORWARD — feel the deep front-of-hip stretch on the kneeling side.
Posterior pelvic tilt — tuck your tailbone under slightly to DEEPEN the stretch dramatically.
Raise the arm on the kneeling side overhead and lean slightly away — adds thoracic component.
Hold 60 seconds minimum. Breathe deeply. You will feel the tension release after ~30 seconds.

▾
🍑 Why this matters: Pigeon pose releases the piriformis and deep hip rotators that sit beneath the gluteus maximus. When these are tight they compress the sciatic nerve and limit hip range of motion — directly reducing how deep and effective your squats and hip thrusts are.

Stand tall. Squeeze both glutes as hard as you can. Hold 5 seconds. Release. Repeat. This establishes the mind-muscle connection before your session. You're essentially "turning on" the nervous pathway to the glute muscle.

Banded Clamshell
Light resistance band just above knees. Lie on side, hips stacked, knees bent 45°. Open the top knee like a clamshell — feel the deep outer hip squeeze. This activates the gluteus medius which stabilizes all your compound movements.
⏱ 20 reps each side · 2 rounds
4
Banded Glute Bridge
Band just above knees. Lie on back, feet flat. Drive hips up. Push knees OUT against the band throughout. Hold 2 seconds at the top per rep. This is your glute activation primer — the exact movement pattern of the hip thrust.
⏱ 20 reps · 2 rounds
5
Banded Lateral Walk
Band above knees. Slight squat position. Walk sideways 10 steps each direction. Keep tension on the band throughout. This fires up the glute medius and hip abductors — essential for knee stability in squats and hip thrusts.
⏱ 10 steps each direction · 3 rounds
6
Bodyweight Hip Thrust Activation
No weight. Perform 15 slow, deliberate hip thrusts. Focus entirely on feeling ONLY your glutes working. This is your rehearsal — perfect the squeeze before you add weight. Your glutes should feel warm and pumped after this.
⏱ 15 reps × 2 rounds

🎯
Mind-Muscle Connection
Before every single set: squeeze your glutes 3 times deliberately. Then do the exercise thinking ONLY about the glute contracting. Research shows this can increase glute activation by 30%+ in the same exercise with the same weight. The mind-muscle connection is a real, measurable phenomenon.
📈
Progressive Overload — The Law
If you're not progressively overloading, you're not growing. Period. Add 2.5kg OR 1 extra rep per week on your main lifts. Log everything. If you can't remember what you lifted last week, you're guessing. This app is your log — use it every session.
😴
Sleep = Muscle Growth
Human Growth Hormone (HGH) peaks during deep sleep — this is the hormone that directly builds muscle. 7–9 hours per night. Every hour less than 7 reduces HGH release, slows recovery, and increases cortisol (which breaks down muscle). Your results are made at night, not in the gym.
💧
3 Litres of Water Daily
Muscle tissue is ~75% water. A 2% drop in hydration causes measurable strength decreases. Drink 3L minimum. Add electrolytes on heavy training days. A hydrated muscle is a stronger, fuller, better-looking muscle.
🍑
Hip Flexor Stretching Daily
If you sit for work, your hip flexors are chronically shortened. Short hip flexors = inhibited glutes. This is called "reciprocal inhibition" — when the hip flexor is tight, the nervous system actually turns DOWN glute activation as a counterbalance. Stretch them EVERY DAY, not just on rest days.
📅
Train with Your Menstrual Cycle
Follicular phase (days 1–14): estrogen is rising, pain tolerance is higher, strength peaks. This is your window for PRs and heavy lifts. Luteal phase (days 15–28): progesterone rises, you may feel stronger or more fatigued — lean into volume and accessory work. Working with your cycle, not against it, means better results and less suffering.
⚡
Rest Periods — Non-Negotiable
Heavy compound sets (hip thrust, deadlift, squat): rest 2.5–3 minutes. Isolation exercises: 60–90 seconds. Shorter rest on compounds = less weight = less growth signal. The rest timer in this app starts automatically when you mark a set done. Use it.
🔢
The Scale Will Lie to You
Muscle is denser than fat. As you build glute muscle and lose fat simultaneously, the scale may barely move — or even go up slightly — while your body completely transforms. TRUST THE TAPE MEASURE. Track your glute circumference every 2 weeks. That number is the truth.
